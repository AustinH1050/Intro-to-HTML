A land-grant institution with more than 35,000 students across a five-campus system, we've graduated more than 240,000 students to serve the state of Oklahoma,
the nation and the world. Students from across the U.S. and more than 100 countries converge on this campus creating a vibrant and inclusive culture. With a student
-to-faculty ratio of 20:1, we will help you prepare for the future by providing the academic support you need. Founded on Dec. 25, 1890 as Oklahoma Agricultural
and Mechanical College, OSU's land-grant mission is to make a college education more accessible. Our roots are set in a rich tradition, and our actions are guided
by the Cowboy Code. Since taking office in 2008, President Burns Hargis and First Cowgirl Ann Hargis have created a campus culture of collaboration, innovation,
wellness and success. Their leadership combined with that of our faculty and staff shapes the various divisions and departments of OSU. Located in northeast
Oklahoma, Stillwater is just 60 miles from both Oklahoma City and Tulsa. Southern Living ranked Stillwater sixth on its list for "The South's Friendliest
Cities 2018," and OSU sits at the heart of it all.
